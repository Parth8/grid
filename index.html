The 2026 reset

EVERYTHING
just changed.

Lewis Hamilton called these the biggest regulation changes of his entire 20-year career. He’s not exaggerating. Engines, chassis, fuel, aerodynamics — all rebuilt from scratch.

01
Active Aero
Front and rear wings now have movable flaps that open on straights to cut drag, snap shut in corners for grip. DRS is gone.
Available to everyone
02
50-50 Hybrid
The engine is now 50% electric, 50% combustion. Triple the battery power versus 2025. The MGU-H is gone.
350kW deployment
03
Sustainable Fuel
100% advanced sustainable fuel — made from non-food bio matter or carbon captured from the air. F1 going Net Zero by 2030.
Drop-in road-ready
04
Smaller, Lighter
Cars are 30kg lighter, 10cm narrower, 20cm shorter. More nimble through tight corners and street circuits.
More agile racing
05
Cadillac arrives
F1’s 11th team — General Motors’ American outfit fronted by veterans Bottas and Perez. The grid grew for the first time in years.
22 cars now
06
Audi works
Audi takes over Sauber as a full works team — chassis and engine. The four rings on the grid for the first time ever.
Ex-Ferrari boss Binotto runs it
07
Manual Override
A push-to-pass button. Manually trigger maximum electrical deployment to attack or defend on track. The new DRS, kind of.
Tactical electricity
08
Madrid debuts
A new street circuit in Madrid replaces Imola. Bahrain and Saudi were cancelled — 22 races instead of 24.
First Spanish street race

Race Director

FLAGS.
the language of the track.

Before radios, flags were how drivers got told what was happening. They’re still the official signaling system today.

GREEN
Track clear. Resume normal racing. Shown when a yellow zone ends.
YELLOW
Danger ahead. No overtaking. Single = caution. Double = be ready to stop.
RED
Session stopped. Back to the pits immediately. Major incident or unsafe conditions.
BLUE
Lapped car — let the leader through. Ignore three blues, get a penalty.
YELLOW + RED
Slippery surface. Oil, water or debris. Extra caution through the flagged area.
CHEQUERED
Session or race over. First driver to cross it in a race wins the Grand Prix.
BLACK
You’re disqualified. Shown with the driver’s number. Done for the day.
BLACK + WHITE
Unsportsmanlike warning. Like a yellow card in football. Next offence brings a penalty.
